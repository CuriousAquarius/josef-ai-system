# Micro-SaaS Scoring Formula
*Source: sukh_saroy on X — validated against 47 real products*

## The 5 Factors (score each 1–5)

1. **Customer income** — Do they have real money to spend?
   - Broke content creators = 1 · Etsy sellers = 3 · Contractors/funeral homes/real estate = 5

2. **Usage frequency** — How often will they use it?
   - Once = 1 · Monthly = 2 · Weekly = 3 · Daily = 5

3. **Existing alternatives** — Is the market saturated?
   - Saturated (ChatGPT exists) = 1 · Moderately competitive = 3 · Real gap exists = 5

4. **Technical sophistication of buyer** — Can they build it themselves?
   - Developers = 1 · Marketers = 3 · Tradespeople/service businesses = 5

5. **Integration depth / stickiness** — Does it get harder to leave over time?
   - Every session independent = 1 · Some lock-in = 3 · Data/history accumulates = 5

## Scoring

**Multiply all 5 scores.**
- Above 500 → Build it
- 200–500 → Consider it
- Below 200 → Skip it

## Example: Serra for local service businesses
5 × 5 × 4 × 5 × 4 = **2,000** ✅ Strong build

## How to apply
Whenever Josef has a new product/tool idea, run it through this formula before building anything.
Claude should run this automatically when Josef pitches a new idea.

## Vertical-first rule (from same source)
Never pitch a tool as generic. Always pitch for a specific industry.
- "A chatbot" = fails
- "A chatbot for funeral homes" = wins
Specific industry = less competition + better retention + higher willingness to pay.
