# Idea Evaluation Framework

Use this to rigorously evaluate any passive income idea before spending time building it.

---

## Stage 1: Problem Validation (Do Before Anything Else)

### The Problem Statement Test
Complete this sentence clearly:
> "[Specific type of person] struggles with [specific problem] which causes [specific pain]. They currently solve it by [current solution] but that's frustrating because [specific frustration]."

If you can't complete it clearly → the idea isn't defined enough yet.

### The Willingness to Pay Test
Find 5 real people who match your target user. Ask them:
1. "Do you have this problem?" (yes/no)
2. "How do you currently solve it?"
3. "Would you pay for a better solution? How much?"
4. "Would you pay right now if I could show you it works?"

**Pass criteria:** At least 3 out of 5 say yes to questions 1 and 3, and give a specific dollar amount.

**If they fail:** The problem isn't painful enough, or you're talking to the wrong people.

## Stage 2: Solution Sizing

### Effort vs. Revenue Matrix

| Effort to Build | Potential Monthly Revenue | Verdict |
|----------------|--------------------------|---------|
| Low (1–2 weeks) | Low ($0–$200) | OK for learning, not sustainable |
| Low (1–2 weeks) | Medium ($200–$1000) | **Sweet spot for beginners** |
| Low (1–2 weeks) | High ($1000+) | Unlikely — if it were this easy, it exists already |
| High (months) | Low ($0–$200) | Avoid |
| High (months) | High ($1000+) | Only after you have experience |

## Stage 3: Market Check

### Competition Analysis
Search for the idea on:
- Google: "[your idea] tool" / "[your idea] app"
- Product Hunt: producthunt.com
- App stores

**If nothing similar exists:** Usually means no market, not a gap. Be very cautious.
**If many similar things exist:** Study them. What do their users complain about? That's your angle.
**If 1–3 similar things exist:** Best scenario — proven market, not yet crowded.

### Pricing Research
Find what similar tools charge. Common patterns:
- Simple automation tools: $5–$20/month
- Productivity tools: $10–$50/month
- Business/professional tools: $50–$200/month
- Enterprise: $200+/month

Your price should be in this range unless you have strong reason to differ.

---

## Stage 4: Distribution Reality Check

Building the tool is usually 20% of the work. Getting users is 80%.

Ask:
- Where do my target users hang out online? (Reddit, Facebook groups, LinkedIn, forums?)
- How will I reach the first 10 users? (Be specific — not "social media")
- Why would they trust something from an unknown person?
- Is there a community I'm already part of that needs this?

**Easiest distribution paths for a beginner:**
1. Build for a community you're already in
2. Find a niche Reddit or Facebook group with this problem → solve it → post there
3. Reach out directly to 20 people who match the profile

---

## Stage 5: MVP Definition

The MVP (Minimum Viable Product) is the smallest thing you can build that proves someone will pay.

### MVP Rules
- Must be buildable in 1–4 weeks with current skills
- Must solve the core problem (not all features)
- Must be ugly enough that you're slightly embarrassed to show it
- Someone must pay real money for it (not "they said they would")

### MVP Template
> "My MVP is: [simple description]. It will do [1-2 core things only]. It will NOT do [list of features to cut]. I'll consider it validated when [X paying users / $Y revenue]."
