# Lead Research Assistant

The Lead Research Assistant helps sales, business development, and marketing professionals identify qualified potential customers by analyzing products/services and finding target companies that match ideal customer profiles.

## Core Capabilities

1. **Business Analysis** — Examines your product/service and value proposition
2. **Company Identification** — Searches for organizations matching your ICP based on industry, size, location, technology, and growth indicators
3. **Lead Prioritization** — Ranks prospects using fit scores (1-10)
4. **Strategic Outreach** — Suggests personalized contact approaches
5. **Data Enrichment** — Gathers details about decision-makers and company context

## When to Use This Skill

- Building a pipeline of potential clients
- Researching whether a company is a good fit before reaching out
- Preparing personalized outreach messages
- Identifying decision-makers at target companies
- Finding "signals of need" (job postings, funding, tech stack, news)

## How to Use

### Basic Lead Research
```
Find leads for [your product/service]. My ideal customer is [description].
```

### Targeted Company Research
```
Is [Company Name] a good fit for [your product]? Score them and tell me who to contact.
```

### ICP-Based Search
```
Find 10 companies in [industry] with [size/location/tech stack] that would benefit from [your product].
```

### Outreach Preparation
```
I'm reaching out to [Company]. Prepare a personalized value proposition and conversation starter.
```

## Output Format

Results include:
- Company overview and fit rationale
- Fit score (1-10) with explanation
- Target decision-maker roles and names (where available)
- Personalized value proposition for each prospect
- Conversation starters based on company context
- Signals of need (recent news, job postings, funding, tech choices)

## Tips for Best Results

- Be specific about your product's unique value
- Define your ideal customer profile clearly (industry, size, location, tech stack, pain points)
- Mention any constraints (geography, company size, budget range)
- Include what problems your product solves — not just what it does

## Example

**User**: "Find leads for Serra, a chatbot-as-a-service for small businesses. Ideal customer: local service businesses (real estate, funeral homes, photographers) who need 24/7 lead capture but can't afford a full-time receptionist."

**Output**: List of 10 qualifying companies with fit scores, decision-maker names, personalized pitches, and outreach starters based on each company's specific context.
